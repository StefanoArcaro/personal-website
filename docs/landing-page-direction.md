# Landing Page — Direction & Ideas

Collected 2026-04-24 from brainstorming session.

## The Problem

The landing page is too empty — just a name, tagline, and two buttons.
It feels like a business card, not a home page.

## What We Know Works (from research)

Best engineer portfolios use the landing page as a curated sampler:
- Hero → About snippet → Featured work → Current interests → Contact
- Progressive disclosure: hook first, details on subpages
- Personality + credibility balance
- Key examples: Brittany Chiang, Lee Robinson, Tomasz Gil

## Layout — Still Open

Haven't settled on single-scroll vs. compact cards vs. something else.
The portal idea changes the layout equation — the bottom of the page
might literally be a transition into pixel art territory.

## Portal Concepts (all three are interesting — a combination could work)

### A: Scroll-driven transition
The page gradually shifts as you scroll past the polished content (about,
work highlights). The background starts showing a pixel grid, typography
gets chunkier, and at the bottom you're looking at a pixel-art doorway.
The scroll IS the portal. No button needed.

### B: Persistent pixel-art element
A small animated scene lives in the corner of the landing page — the
trainer walking around a tiny room. Click it to "zoom in" and enter the
world. It's always visible, hinting that something else lives underneath
the polished surface.

### C: The floor becomes the world
Scroll to the bottom and the page floor becomes the overworld. No button,
no label. The world is just... there. You discover it.

### Combination idea
Could blend all three: a small persistent pixel-art hint (B) that becomes
more prominent as you scroll (A), until the bottom of the page IS the
overworld (C). The entire experience is one continuous transition from
polished to pixel.
